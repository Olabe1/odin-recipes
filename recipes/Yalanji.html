Vegetarian Stuffed Grape Leaves (Yalanji) Recipes

Home

Description

Yalanji is a traditional Middle Eastern dish made from grape leaves filled with a fragrant mixture of rice, herbs, and spices. These vegetarian stuffed grape leaves are gently rolled by hand and slowly cooked until tender. They have a fresh, tangy flavor from lemon juice and olive oil, with a soft texture that melts in your mouth. Yalanji is often served cold or at room temperature and is a popular dish for family gatherings, holidays, and special occasions.

Ingredients

Stuffing Ingredients

  • One jar of Grape Leaves Al-Afia, rinse the leaves
  • 2 cups White rice preferably short grain such as calrose or Egyptian rice but long grain works too, soaked & rinsed
  • 1 Bunch of parsley
  • 2 Roma tomatoes
  • 1 Red bell pepper
  • 1 Yellow onion
  • 4 Garlic cloves
  • 4 Garlic cloves
  • 1.5 Lemon's juice
  • 11/2 tbsp Allspice powder
  • 1 tbsp Pomegranate molasses
  • 1 tbsp Dried mint
  • 1 tbsp Shatta (Arab chili paste) sambal works too or omit (it is optional)
  • 1 tbsp Tomato paste
  • Salt to taste Salt a decent amount-it will bring out the flavors. at least 1 tsp.

Cooking Liquid for the Grape Leaves

  • 3 cups Water boiling
  • 1 Bouillon cube
  • 1 lemon’s juice
  • 1 1/2 tbsp Tomato paste
  • Drizzle of pomegranate molasses
  • 2 tbsp Olive oil
  • Salt & Pepper to taste
  • 4 Cardamom pods

Steps

  1. Pulse the vegetables (except the tomatoes) in a food processor until finely chopped. Add tomatoes and pulse briefly a couple of times.
  2. Combine the chopped vegetables with the soaked rice. Season with salt, pepper, allspice, dried mint, tomato paste, lemon juice, olive oil, and pomegranate molasses. Mix well.
  3. Let the filling sit for a few minutes so the flavors blend. You can also prepare it ahead and refrigerate it.
  4. Prepare the grape leaves: rinse jarred leaves well and remove any tough stems. If using fresh leaves, blanch them in hot water until softened.
  5. Place one leaf on a flat surface with the veiny side facing up. Add a small line of filling near the bottom of the leaf.
  6. Fold the bottom over the filling, fold in the sides, then roll into a tight cylinder. Keep it snug but not so tight that the rice can’t expand.
  7. Line the bottom of a pot with extra leaves (and optionally tomato or potato slices). Arrange the rolls tightly in layers so they don’t open while cooking.
  8. Mix the cooking liquid ingredients, then pour it over the rolls until they are just covered. Place a small plate on top to keep everything in place.
  9. Bring the pot to a gentle boil, then reduce heat, cover, and simmer until the leaves are tender and the rice is cooked.
  10. Let the pot cool slightly, then serve with lemon wedges. Yogurt on the side is a common option.
